About

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Materials Science and Engineering at Penn State.

Contact MeFormal CVLinkedIn

I started my Materials Engineering journey at Iowa State University where I specialized in metals and electronic materials. I spent two summers as an undergrad at NCSU researching electroceramic materials in both bulk and thin film forms.

I received my Master's in Materials Science and Engineering at Penn State in 2016 studying ferroelectric thin films and their properties. I returned to NCSU after my Master's to pursue a Ph.D., studying thin film deposition of carbides. After 1 year, our lab moved to Penn State.

My current research focuses on the investigation of high entropy ultra high temperature ceramic materials by studying their fundamental properties in thin film form. Our primary materials of interest are early tranisition metal carbides and nitrides.

Future Goals

Equipment Engineer / Applications Scientist

Both in my Master's and Doctoral careers I have developed a passion for developing or modifying equipment for a desired purpose as well as teaching others how to use complex pieces of equipment and finding it's limits. I would greatly enjoy a career that involves either developing complex scientific equipment, learning the capabilities of new equipment, or teaching end-users how to complete the tasks they wish to complete.

User Facilities Lab

Whether at a national lab or a university working in a user facilities lab would, in a similar vein to the prior interests, allow me to both teach end-users how to use complex equipment as well as develop new training protocols and nuances to the technique

Process Engineer

Having the opportunity to develop new materials deposition processes (a task I was frequently tasked with in my doctoral studies), as well as troubleshoot and look for creative solutions to get a process back to the necessary specifications.
